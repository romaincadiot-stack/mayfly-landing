What is a transit?

The time an aircraft spends on the ground, from arrival to departure. That's the unit Mayfly is built around.

Every role sees the same data, at the same moment, on any device.

Ground dispatcher, passenger agent, ramp agent, supervisor: one shared picture.

From touchdown to pushback, everything orchestrated in one place.

During the turnaround
01 / Real-time flight view

See every flight, in the state it's really in

Flights are organised by what they need right now: inbound, on the ground, or departed. Each one shows its flight number, airline, aircraft, route, scheduled and estimated times, and stand. Status moves on its own as the turnaround progresses.

  • Opens straight onto active flights, falling back to arrivals when the apron is quiet
  • Refreshes continuously with a live connection indicator you can trust
On ground • 3 Arrivals • 3 Departed • 3 Live
02 / Operational milestones (A-CDM)

One timestamp updates everyone

Agents log the key moments of the turnaround: landing, block on, unloading, cleaning, boarding, doors, loading, block off, take-off. Correct a mistaken entry in a tap. The rest follows on its own.

  • Estimated departure recomputed from the real block-on and each airline's minimum ground time
  • Flight advances state automatically and pushes the change to everyone on the team

03 / Team coordination

A crew on every flight, no phone tag

Supervisors assign a ground dispatcher, a passenger agent, a ramp agent and an operations agent to each transit. Agents only see the flights that are theirs; supervisors see the whole station.

  • A dedicated message thread per flight, visible only to its crew and supervisors
  • Flight notes to record a delay, a special operation or an airline instruction

04 / Passenger operations

From check-in counts to the final figures

The passenger agent enters booked, checked-in and bag counts, confirms the high-load estimate once check-in closes, and locks the final figures before departure.

  • Track wheelchairs, unaccompanied minors, reduced mobility, animals and medical needs in one place
  • Load recommendations re-run the moment a count changes, with a live boarding countdown

06 / Stand management

Right aircraft, right stand, right time

A schedule view lays out every stand over time and flags conflicts before they bite. Assign by drag-and-drop, respect each stand's constraints, or ask for an automatic layout that makes the most of the apron.

  • Alerts for overlapping windows, wrong aircraft size, night restrictions
  • Automatic stand suggestion to optimize apron usage across the day

Everything else the station runs on

Ground service requests

Raise fuelling, cleaning, de-icing, water or other requests from the flight, set a priority, and the operations team accepts or declines in real time.

Team planning

A lane per agent shows their flights across the day, colour-coded by role, so supervisors can read workload at a glance and rebalance before anyone is overloaded.

Airport map

See the airport layout with stands and live aircraft positions. Taxiways fade out as you zoom to keep the picture readable on a tablet in the field.

IATA delay codes

The full IATA delay-code reference, with operational descriptions and each airline's preferred set, on hand from any screen without leaving what you're doing.

Multi-station

Each airport is an isolated station with its own data, users and configuration. One platform runs many stations at once; platform admins see across all of them.

Access by role

Everyone sees what they need, nothing more

Roles scope what each person can see and do. Station admins manage users, airlines and airport configuration; platform admins oversee every station.
